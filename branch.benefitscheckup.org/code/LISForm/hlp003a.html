Help: Find Out If You Qualify: Part 1

Have you or your spouse worked this year?

If you or your spouse have worked at any time during the present calendar year (1/1/2005 to the present), select Yes for this question. If you have not worked at any time during the current calendar year, select No.

Are you or your spouse UNDER age 65?

If you or your spouse are under age 65, disabled or blind, and working, we may be able to exclude some of your earnings when we determine your eligibility for help with prescription drug plan costs. If you spend part of your earnings to pay for things that you need in order to work, we will not count that part of your earnings when we determine whether you are eligible for this help. For example, we would exclude the amount you spend on attendant care, certain drugs, medical supplies and devices, certain types of training and therapy, certain work-related equipment, etc.

How many relatives who live with you and your spouse depend on you or your spouse to provide at least one-half of their financial support? Do NOT include yourself or your spouse in this number.

Eligibility for the extra help is based on the amount of your income and that of your spouse compared to the Federal Poverty Level for your household's size. Therefore, we need to know how many relatives are in your household for whom you or your spouse provide at least one-half of their financial support.

Enter the number of relatives who live with you and your spouse and who depend on you or your spouse for at least one-half of their financial support. Do NOT include yourself or your spouse in this number.

Does anyone provide, or help you or your spouse pay for any of the following household expenses?

If anyone regularly provides you or your spouse with assistance with food or shelter costs, select Yes. If you occasionally receive help, such as your child buying groceries for the week, do NOT include that amount.

If Yes, also enter the average amount you receive each month in the space provided.
