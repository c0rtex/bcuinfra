Successful Submission - Print Or Save Your Receipt

The Application For Help With Medicare Prescription Drug Plan Costs was received by Social Security on April 14, 2006, 9:55AM Eastern Daylight Time

Congratulations! You have completed the Application for Help with Medicare Prescription Drug Plan Costs. If you qualify for this extra help, it should cover between 85 and 100 percent of your prescription costs.

Following this letter, you will see your Successful Submission Receipt from Social Security. Please be sure to hold onto this receipt in case you need to check on the status of your application.

What happens next with your application:

Social Security will process your application as quickly as possible. They will contact you if they need more information. When they finish processing your application, Social Security will send you a letter to let you know if you qualify for extra help. They will also send you information about the Medicare Prescription Drug Coverage.

If you do not hear back from Social Security about your application within the next month, you can contact them at 1-800-772-1213 to check on your application. They should be able to look up your application either by your name or your Social Security number.

What you need to do:

Carefully read the letter Social Security sends you. The information they provide will tell you what you should do next. You also should hold onto the Successful Submission Receipt, as you may need it in order to enroll in a plan before November 15, 2006.

Remember, if you qualify for this extra help, you must enroll in a Medicare prescription drug plan in order to get prescription drug coverage through Medicare.

If you qualify for the extra help and you do not choose a Medicare prescription drug plan on your own, Medicare will choose one for you to be sure you are covered. But, if you wait for Medicare to enroll you, you may have months for which you do not have prescription drug coverage.

For information about prescription drug plans in your area, you may call toll-free 1-800-MEDICARE (1-800-633-4227) or visit www.medicare.gov. If you are deaf or hard of hearing, you may call the Medicare TTY number toll-free at 1-877-486-2048.
